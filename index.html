About White Box

White Box is a browser extension for Chrome and Edge that filters sexually explicit or inappropriate content from your browsing experience. Designed for families, educators, and anyone who wants more control over what appears on screen, White Box empowers you to craft your own safe corner of the internet.

Features

  • Filters explicit images and text on the fly
  • Customizable blocklists and allowlists
  • Works offline – no external servers needed, all filtering is done locally
  • Easily disable or enable with a single click
  • Supports Chrome and Microsoft Edge
  • Free version for basic filtering; Pro version adds advanced features and updates

What Sets White Box Apart

Unlike traditional filters that rely solely on predefined blocklists of domains or URLs, White Box actively scans the content of every page you visit and makes intelligent decisions about what you see. It uses a mix of heuristics and AI methods to detect and white‑box unwanted content—including images and text—on sites that are not blocked by normal filters. This means you’re protected even on new or obscure sites, and you stay in control of your browsing experience.

Current Version

White Box is currently in an early access beta. It offers three filtering levels—None, Moderate and Strict—so you can choose how much content you want to block. Moderate mode balances protection and experience, while Strict mode aggressively filters questionable content for maximum safety. Future versions will add more customization, smarter detection and a streamlined workflow. Learn more about this release and our roadmap on the version page.

Installation & Setup

Installing on Google Chrome

1. Visit the White Box extension page on the Chrome Web Store.
2. Click Add to Chrome and confirm the installation when prompted.
3. After it installs, pin the White Box icon from the extensions menu for quick access.

Installing on Microsoft Edge

1. Go to the Microsoft Edge Add‑ons store and search for “White Box”.
2. Click Get (or Add to Edge) and approve the prompts to install.
3. Once installed, ensure White Box is enabled in your Edge extensions list.
